Tribute to Sarojini Naidu

The Nightingale of India

Sarojini Naidu, born on February 13, 1879, in Hyderabad, was a remarkable poet, politician, and advocate for Indian independence. Known as the Nightingale of India, she used her literary genius and eloquent speeches to fight for India's freedom and women’s rights.

Her Early Life & Education

Sarojini Naidu demonstrated extraordinary intelligence and talent from a young age. She wrote her first play at 12 and went on to study at King’s College in London. Her experiences abroad deepened her passion for India's independence, influencing her work both as a writer and activist.

Key Achievements

  • First woman to become President of the Indian National Congress
  • Leader of the Indian Independence Movement
  • Advocate for women’s rights and education
  • Renowned poet with published works like *The Golden Threshold*

A Glimpse of Her Poetry

“Awake, my heart, to be loved, awake, awake!
The darkness silvers away, the morn doth break.
It’s the dawn of a new India, and we must arise
With courage, with strength, with hope in our eyes.”
